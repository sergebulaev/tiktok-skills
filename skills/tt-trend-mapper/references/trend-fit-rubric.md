# Trend Fit Rubric and Lifecycle Examples

A scored version of the four-question fit check, plus worked examples of riding,
bending, and skipping a trend.

## The scored fit check

Score each question 0, 1, or 2. A trend needs **6 or more out of 8** to be worth
riding. Below 6, recommend skipping.

| Question | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Niche fit** | no connection beyond "it trended" | a stretch you can justify | obvious why you did it |
| **Timing** | saturated or dead | peaking, big accounts on it | emerging, first 1-3 days |
| **Native structure** | you would ignore the format | you would loosely follow it | you can hit the beat exactly |
| **Twist potential** | nothing only your niche would add | a mild variation | a turn that is unmistakably yours |

- **6-8:** ride it. The reach boost is real and the ride will read as yours.
- **4-5:** only ride with a strong twist, and expect modest lift.
- **0-3:** skip. The cost (looking late or forced) outweighs the borrowed reach.

## Why skipping is a feature

Every forced trend ride spends trust. An audience can tell when a creator chased
a sound that had nothing to do with them. Saying "this one is not for us" and
posting your own thing keeps the niche sharp. A trend is a tool, not an
obligation.

## Worked example 1: ride it (score 8)

- **Trend:** an emerging sound where the audio says "tell me you are X without
  telling me you are X", climbing for two days.
- **Niche:** a backend engineering creator.
- **Niche fit (2):** obvious, "tell me you are a backend dev without telling me".
- **Timing (2):** two days in, still emerging.
- **Native structure (2):** the format is a quick montage on the beat; easy to hit.
- **Twist (2):** montage of cursed production logs, a 3am pager alert, a 400-line
  YAML file. Unmistakably this niche.
- **Verdict:** ride now, T10 hook, cut the montage on the beat drop.

## Worked example 2: bend it hard (score 5)

- **Trend:** a peaking dance sound, on every big lifestyle account.
- **Niche:** a SaaS founder who does not dance.
- **Niche fit (1):** a stretch.
- **Timing (1):** peaking, not emerging.
- **Native structure (2):** clear beat.
- **Twist (1):** mild.
- **Verdict:** only ride if the twist gets sharper. Better move: use the sound but
  subvert the format (sit perfectly still while the caption lists what you would
  rather do than dance), turning "I do not do this trend" into the joke.

## Worked example 3: skip it (score 2)

- **Trend:** a saturated meme sound, three weeks old, declining.
- **Niche:** a finance educator.
- **Niche fit (0):** none.
- **Timing (0):** saturated and falling.
- **Native structure (1):** loose.
- **Twist (1):** nothing original.
- **Verdict:** skip. Riding it now reads as late and off-brand. Post a native
  finance hook instead (T3 Specific-Number Reveal).

## Original audio: the long game

A sound you create that other people reuse becomes its own distribution channel
back to your profile. When a niche has no good trending sound to ride, making a
reusable original (a repeatable line, a sound bite, a format) can outperform
chasing someone else's. It is slower but compounds.
