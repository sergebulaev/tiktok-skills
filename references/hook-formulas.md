# 10 TikTok 3-Second Hook Formulas - 2026 Edition

On TikTok the hook is not a caption. It is the **first 1 to 3 seconds of the
video**: the spoken opening line, the first on-screen text, and the opening
visual, all firing at once. The viewer decides to stay or swipe before second
three. Nothing else in the video matters if the hook does not win.

Each formula below has three layers (spoken line, on-screen text, opening
visual), why it works, and a primary engagement goal. The goals map to TikTok's
ranked signals in 2026:

| Goal | What it earns | Why it matters on TikTok |
|---|---|---|
| **completion** | watch to the last frame | completion rate is the single heaviest ranked signal; a short video watched fully beats a long one watched halfway |
| **saves** | saves + rewatches + shares (TikTok's "save" analog) | the strongest quality signal after completion; reference-worthy and replayable content gets saved and resent |
| **comments** | back-and-forth in the comments | a real comment thread tells the ranker the video sparked conversation |
| **shares** | sends to a friend, reposts | a share re-injects the video into a brand-new follower graph |
| **follows** | profile follow from the video | the long-term compounding signal; one follow is worth many likes |

Formulas T1 to T6 fit any single video. T7 to T10 are shapes for slightly longer
or serial videos. The spoken line and the on-screen text should differ: many
viewers watch the first second muted, so the text has to carry the promise even
with the sound off.

> No em dashes in any spoken line or on-screen card. Use `..` as a soft pause if
> you need one. A dash on screen reads as a typo.

## T1 - Cold-Open Result

**Goal: completion.** Open on the finished outcome, then promise the how.

```
Spoken:    this took me {specific number} tries and {timeframe}. here is the one that worked.
On-screen: {the result, stated flat} in {timeframe}
Visual:    the finished result on screen in the first frame, no intro
```

**Why:** Showing the payoff first makes the viewer stay to see how it was done.
The number sets the value before they can swipe. There is no logo, no greeting,
no warm-up to scroll past.

**Warning:** The result has to be real and visible in frame. A promised result you
never show on screen reads as bait and tanks completion.

## T2 - Pattern Interrupt

**Goal: completion.** Break the expected frame in the first second so the thumb
stops.

```
Spoken:    stop {common thing the viewer is doing}. you are doing it backwards.
On-screen: you are doing {thing} wrong
Visual:    an unexpected motion or object that does not match the niche, then snap to you
```

**Why:** The scroll is a trance. A visual or verbal interrupt (an out-of-place
object, an abrupt "stop") breaks it long enough for the promise to land. The
"backwards / wrong" frame opens a loop the viewer needs closed.

**Warning:** The interrupt has to pay off. A shock open with a boring middle
trains viewers to swipe your next one fast.

## T3 - Specific-Number Reveal

**Goal: saves.** Lead with one odd, concrete number that reframes the topic.

```
Spoken:    {odd-precision number} {what it measures}. most people guess way off.
On-screen: {odd-precision number} {unit}
Visual:    the number big on screen, then cut to the proof
```

**Why:** A single hard number is the most save-worthy unit on TikTok. People save
the video to act on the number later, and a save plus a rewatch is the platform's
"this was worth it" signal. Odd figures (47 minutes, 2.4x, $873) read as real.

**Warning:** Never invent the number. A figure that gets fact-checked in the
comments costs more reach than the hook earned.

## T4 - Open Loop Question

**Goal: comments.** Ask the exact question the video will answer, out loud, in
the first two seconds.

```
Spoken:    why does {surprising thing} happen? i tested it so you do not have to.
On-screen: why does {surprising thing} happen?
Visual:    you mid-gesture, caught at the moment of the question
```

**Why:** A named question opens a loop and invites the viewer to guess in the
comments before the reveal. Comments early in a video's life pull more reach, and
the answer keeps them to the end (completion).

**Warning:** Answer the question inside the video. A loop you never close reads as
clickbait and earns the angry "so what was it" comments that do not help.

## T5 - Relatable Call-Out

**Goal: shares.** Name a specific shared experience so the viewer thinks of one
person to send it to.

```
Spoken:    if you {very specific relatable behavior}, this video is calling you out.
On-screen: pov: you {specific relatable moment}
Visual:    a re-enactment of the exact moment, first frame
```

**Why:** Recognition is instant and shareable. "Send this to the friend who does
this" is the most common share trigger on TikTok, and a share re-injects the video
into a new graph. The more specific the behavior, the stronger the send.

**Warning:** Generic relatability disappears. "When you are tired" is nothing.
"When you reopen the fridge for the third time hoping new food appeared" lands.

## T6 - Bold Claim, No Hedge

**Goal: comments.** State a flat, defensible, against-the-grain claim with zero
qualifier.

```
Spoken:    {flat contrarian claim, no "i think", no "maybe"}.
On-screen: {the claim, 4 to 7 words}
Visual:    you straight to camera, still, holding the frame
```

**Why:** A confident claim with no hedge invites the comment section to argue,
and the argument is the reach. Stillness in the visual (no cuts) signals you mean
it. The viewer stays to see if you back it up.

**Warning:** Only ship a claim you would defend in person. A bold take you cannot
support gets dragged, and TikTok comment fights can be brutal.

## T7 - Listicle Promise

**Goal: saves.** Promise a numbered payoff up front, then deliver one item per
beat with on-screen counters.

```
Spoken:    {N} {things} that {specific payoff}. number {X} surprised even me.
On-screen: {N} {things} for {payoff}
Visual:    the counter "1 / {N}" appears in the corner from the first second
```

**Why:** The numbered promise plus "number X surprised me" opens a loop the
viewer rides to the end. A reference list is heavy save-bait, and a viewer who
rewatches to catch item 3 hands TikTok two strong signals at once. Put the
strongest item early; attention decays with each beat.

**Warning:** If the hook promises 7 and the video gives 4 weak ones, you train
viewers to skip your lists. The promise is a contract.

## T8 - Story In Medias Res

**Goal: completion.** Drop the viewer into the peak of a real story, mid-action,
no setup.

```
Spoken:    so i am {in the worst moment of the story}, and then {raise the stakes}.
On-screen: this almost {disaster}
Visual:    the most dramatic frame of the story first, then rewind
```

**Why:** Starting at the tension skips the warm-up the scroll punishes. The viewer
stays to learn how it resolves, and a story watched to the turn earns the highest
completion. The "rewind to how we got here" device is a proven retention loop.

**Warning:** Only use a true story. Manufactured stakes get caught, and a faked arc
destroys trust fast in a niche audience.

---

## T9 - Tutorial Cold Start

**Goal: saves.** Skip the intro and start the how-to on the first action, with the
end result previewed.

```
Spoken:    here is how to {specific result} in {timeframe}. step one, right now.
On-screen: how to {result} (save this)
Visual:    a 1-second flash of the finished result, then cut straight to step one
```

**Why:** A no-intro tutorial with the result previewed is the most-saved format on
TikTok: viewers save it to follow along later, and that save plus a rewatch is the
"this is useful" signal. The 1-second result preview is the promise; the immediate
step-one is the proof you will deliver.

**Warning:** Vague steps ("just be consistent") kill the save. Every step needs a
concrete, do-it-now instruction.

## T10 - Trend-Ride With A Twist

**Goal: shares.** Take a currently trending sound or format and bend it to your
niche in the first second, so it is familiar and surprising at once.

```
Spoken:    {the trending audio's setup line}, but for {your niche}.
On-screen: {the trend's expected text} .. except {your niche twist}
Visual:    the recognizable trend beat, then the twist your audience did not expect
```

**Why:** A trending sound carries built-in reach; the twist is what makes it yours
and makes it shareable inside your niche. Riding the familiar beat earns the
algorithm's trend boost while the twist earns the share ("they did the trend but
for us"). See `algorithm-heuristics.md` on sound selection and the trend window.

**Warning:** A trend you copy with no twist is forgettable and reads as
late-to-it. And do not force a trend that clashes with your niche; a cringe ride
costs more trust than skipping the trend. See `tt-trend-mapper`.

## Engagement-goal split

Pick the formula by what you want the video to earn, then narrow by topic.

| Goal | Earned by | Formulas |
|---|---|---|
| **completion** | cold-open results, pattern interrupts, stories started at the tension | T1 Cold-Open Result, T2 Pattern Interrupt, T8 Story In Medias Res |
| **saves** | hard numbers, reference lists, no-intro tutorials | T3 Specific-Number, T7 Listicle Promise, T9 Tutorial Cold Start |
| **comments** | open-loop questions, bold un-hedged claims | T4 Open Loop Question, T6 Bold Claim |
| **shares** | hyper-specific relatable call-outs, niche trend twists | T5 Relatable Call-Out, T10 Trend-Ride |
| **follows** | any of the above with a reason to expect more (a series, a clear niche) | T7, T9 framed as part of a series |

## Hook micro-rules (TikTok-specific)

- **Spoken line and on-screen text differ.** The text carries the promise muted;
  the voice carries it with sound. Saying the same words twice wastes the second.
- **The result or the tension goes in frame one.** No logo, no greeting, no "hey
  guys". Dead air in the first second is the top completion killer.
- **One specific number in the hook** raises completion and save rate. "47
  minutes" beats "a while".
- **Open the loop, never close it in the hook.** The hook promises; the body
  delivers. A hook that answers itself gives no reason to stay.
- **Design the last frame to restart the loop.** A rewatch counts almost like a
  second view. End on a line or visual that makes the first frame land differently
  the second time.

## Never do

- Open on a greeting, a logo, or a slow zoom. The first second is the whole game.
- Read the caption out loud as the hook. The script and the caption are two jobs.
- Promise a result you never show on screen.
- Force a trending sound onto a niche it does not fit (see `tt-trend-mapper`).
- Stack five calls to action. One clear ask: save, comment, or follow.
